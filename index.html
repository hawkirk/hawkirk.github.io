About Me


Hello! My name is harrison kirk.

I’m a data analyst with experience in the public and nonprofit sectors, and I currently work at Education Analytics on the analytics team. My goal is to use data to develop policy solutions for issues regarding equity in public health and education.

I’m a skilled programmer with a preference for R, but I’ve also worked with SQL, Python, and SAS. My skills include data manipulation and cleaning, web scraping, data visualization, dashboarding, and statistical modeling. I also like playing around with web development and design tools, Linux, and experimenting with new technologies along the way.

I graduated in 2021 with a bachelor's in political science with a minor in statistics from Cal Poly, San Luis Obispo. Outside of class, I was a data analyst intern for the San Luis Obispo County Behavioral Health Department, a data journalist for Mustang News, an editor for Paideia, and a volunteer field researcher at SLO Bangers Syringe Exchange and Overdose Prevention Program. I also co-wrote and lobbied for a California Senate Bill that became a law in 2021. Together, these experiences seeded my passion for data-driven policy research.

When I’m not working, I love exploring nature, creative writing, travel, shooting film photography, and producing music. I’m an avid fan of hip hop - my top five all-time list is Kendrick Lamar, André 3000, Nas, Lil Wayne, and Yasiin Be.

This website is a work in progress at the moment, but I’m hopeful one day this can be a place to showcase my professional and creative projects.
